FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before purchasing.

Is the WH-1000XM5 better than the XM4?
+
Yes and no — it depends on your priorities. The XM5 has significantly better noise cancellation (new dual processors + 8 mics vs 4 mics), improved call quality, a more comfortable fit, and slightly better midrange clarity. However, the XM4 can fold completely flat (better portability), costs ~$100 less, and was still among the best ANC headphones made. If you travel frequently and care about the absolute best ANC, get the XM5. If budget and portability matter more, the XM4 remains excellent.
Can I use them while charging?
+
Yes. You can use the WH-1000XM5 while it charges via USB-C. If you run out of battery, you can also use them passively with the included 3.5mm audio cable — though ANC will not work in wired mode without power.
Do they work with Google Assistant and Alexa?
+
Yes. The WH-1000XM5 has a dedicated button that can be assigned to either Google Assistant or Amazon Alexa in the Sony Headphones Connect app. It also supports hands-free "Hey Google" and "Alexa" voice activation without pressing any button.
Is LDAC worth enabling? Does it drain battery faster?
+
LDAC transmits up to 990 kbps vs standard Bluetooth's 328 kbps — so the audio quality improvement is real and noticeable with high-bitrate source files and a compatible streaming service (Amazon Music Unlimited, Tidal HiFi, etc.). However, it does increase power consumption slightly, reducing battery life by approximately 2-3 hours and may cause more connection drops in congested wireless environments. For casual listening, AAC is more stable. For focused listening sessions with high-res content, LDAC is outstanding.
How does the ANC compare to Bose QuietComfort Ultra?
+
In most real-world tests, the Sony WH-1000XM5 edges out the Bose QC Ultra for overall noise cancellation effectiveness — particularly for mid-to-high frequency noise (voices, keyboard sounds, HVAC hum). The Bose QC Ultra has a slight edge in handling very low-frequency rumble (like aircraft drone). The QC Ultra also has better spatial audio (Bose Immersive Audio) and is slightly more natural-sounding, but costs $30 more. For pure ANC, Sony is #1. For sound stage and spatial feel, Bose is arguably better.
What's in the box?
+
The WH-1000XM5 comes with: the headphones, a premium hard-shell carrying case, a USB-C charging cable (USB-A to USB-C), a 3.5mm audio cable for wired use, and a documentation booklet. Notably, the case is slightly larger than the XM4's case due to the non-folding design.
Are they comfortable for glasses wearers?
+
Generally yes. The XM5 ear cups are large and deep with soft synthetic leather pads, which accommodate most glasses frames without excessive pressure. However, very thick glasses arms can create a small gap that slightly reduces ANC effectiveness and causes minor discomfort over long sessions. Sony has improved the softness and depth of the ear pads specifically for glasses compatibility compared to the XM4.
